In midnight dreary, as I sat alone,

My thoughts a fading light, my heart a stone.

From shadows deep, a raven then appeared,

With eyes like coal, and feathers jagged and weird.


"Nevermore," it seemed to whisper low,

A haunting word, a specter's grim show.

And there it perched, upon my chamber door,

A dark and eerie bird, that filled me with dread core.


With each cawing call, my soul grew weak,

A prisoner's fate, which I could but speak.

And so I sat, with darkness cloying me,

My heart olden, and my spirit finally free.


And as I watched, this raven black and cold,

I knew my fate, forever to unfold.

For in its beady gaze, I saw the mark of death,

A final breath, that would end my fragile breath.


I waited there, with this eerie bird of Poe,

My heart aching, my soul a'quiver and slow.

And in the end, I gave it all away,

My life, my love, in one final spray.


Thus ends my tale, a poem of Poe's design,

Of ravens, death, and sorrow's endless line.

A haunting work, that echoes through the years,

A legacy that still echoes loud, and near.

Anonymous
